Why I Built Quoted

The real story behind building an AI that learns how contractors price—and why I think it matters.

Eddie San Juan January 2026 7 min read

A few years ago, I watched my friend lose a $12,000 job because he took four days to send a quote.

He's a fantastic contractor. Twenty years in the business. Beautiful tile work. The kind of guy who takes pride in every detail. But he was running three jobs at once, and by the time he sat down to write up the estimate, the homeowner had already signed with someone else.

"I knew the numbers," he told me. "I just didn't have time to put it together."

That conversation stuck with me.

The Gap I Kept Seeing

Over the next year, I talked to more contractors. Not just my friend—dozens of them. Plumbers, electricians, painters, general contractors. I wanted to understand how they ran their businesses.

What I found was a pattern: the ones who were great at their trade were often terrible at the business side. Not because they were incapable—because they didn't have time. They were too busy doing the actual work.

The contractors who are best at their trade are often worst at finding time to quote jobs.

Quoting was the bottleneck. Every contractor I talked to had the same story: jobs lost to slow quotes, evenings spent writing estimates, weekends catching up on paperwork. The administrative burden was crushing people who just wanted to build things.

Why Existing Tools Didn't Work

I looked at the software that existed. There were plenty of options. Quoting apps, invoicing tools, CRM systems. But they all had the same problem: they didn't actually save time.

They were just fancier ways to type numbers into boxes.

You still had to figure out the price. You still had to write the scope of work. You still had to manually create each quote from scratch. The software just made the typing look prettier.

For a contractor standing in a customer's kitchen at 6pm, trying to give a price before heading to the next job, that wasn't helpful. They needed something that could think with them, not just transcribe what they already knew.

The AI Question

Around this time, AI was getting genuinely good. Not the hype-cycle stuff—actually useful. I'd been building software for years, and for the first time, it felt like AI could do more than party tricks.

So I started asking: what if AI could learn how a specific contractor prices jobs?

Not generic pricing. Not "industry averages." The actual way this particular electrician thinks about panel upgrades. The factors this specific painter considers for exterior jobs. The logic that lives in their head, accumulated over years of experience.

The insight

Every experienced contractor has a pricing model in their head. They just can't articulate it in a way a computer understands. What if the computer could learn it by watching them work?

That's where Quoted started. Not as a "quoting app" but as an attempt to capture expertise.

Building Something Different

The first version was rough. Really rough. But something interesting happened when contractors used it.

A bathroom remodel that usually took 30 minutes to quote? Done in 60 seconds. And not because the AI was guessing—because it had learned from the contractor's previous quotes. It knew that this contractor charges more for second-floor bathrooms. It knew they add a premium for older homes with potential hidden issues. It had absorbed the wisdom that takes years to develop.

Contractors started telling me: "It's like having someone who knows how I think."

That's when I knew we were onto something.

What We're Actually Building

Quoted isn't about replacing contractor judgment. That would be both impossible and wrong. The goal has always been to amplify it.

When you describe a job to Quoted—by voice, by text, however you want—it's drawing on everything you've taught it about how you price. Your materials preferences. Your labor rates. Your approach to contingencies. The subtle factors that make your quotes yours.

Then it generates a professional quote in seconds. Not a template. A quote that reflects your expertise, formatted professionally, ready to send before you've left the job site.

The AI isn't doing the thinking. You did the thinking—over years of experience. The AI just makes it fast.

Why This Matters

Here's what I believe: contractors are the backbone of the economy. The people who build, fix, and maintain the physical world deserve tools as good as what office workers have.

For too long, "business software" meant software for people who sit at desks. Contractors got stuck with tools designed for a different world—tools that assumed you had time to sit down and type, time to learn complex interfaces, time to manage administrative overhead.

That's backwards. The people with the least time for paperwork are the ones who need the best tools.

Quoted is my attempt to fix that. To give contractors the same technological leverage that's been transforming other industries. To let expertise scale.

Where We're Going

We're still early. The product keeps getting better as more contractors use it and teach it how they work. Every quote makes the system smarter—not just for that contractor, but (in anonymized ways) for everyone.

The vision is a world where quoting takes seconds, not hours. Where every contractor can be as professionally polished as a big company, while staying as agile as an independent. Where the administrative burden that crushes small businesses just... disappears.

We're not there yet. But every day, we get a little closer.

A Note to Contractors

If you've read this far, you're probably wondering if this thing actually works.

The honest answer: it works really well for some jobs and okay for others. The more you use it, the better it gets. Some contractors are generating quotes in under a minute that used to take them 30 minutes. Others take a few weeks to train it before they see that level of accuracy.

It's not magic. It's software that learns. And like any learning system, the quality of the output depends on the quality of the input.

If you're skeptical, that's fine. You should be skeptical of anyone promising to solve your problems with AI. But if you're curious—if you've ever wished you could quote faster without sacrificing accuracy—I think it's worth a look.

The worst case is you waste five minutes on the demo. The best case is you get your evenings back.
